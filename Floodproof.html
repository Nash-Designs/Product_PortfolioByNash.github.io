FloodProof Backpack

This product represents a solution for an ongoing problem in Malaysia.
I chose this product design for my Final Year Project.

Oct 2024 - July 2025

The Report

Thorough research is done to make all the design choices and every step is included in the report. I conducted a survey for my target audience to understand the user needs and also interviewed a flood relief team (CREST Malaysia) that gave me a better understanding of the conditions the product was going to be used in. The report includes all the materials, specifications, and design elements considered for the backpack. The report uses an APA 17th edition format.

Concept Sketches

I mapped out my ideations into sketches and then concepts to showcase what I had in mind for the design of the backpack. The concepts are an early rendition of the final design and the final design will intrgrate few elements from the concepts.

Why I chose to design this backpack?

As an international student, I wanted to try my hand at solving a major crisis in Malaysia. As it turns out, flooding has been a major cause of damages and losses in multiple parts of this country on a yearly basis. So, I took it upon myself to design a floodproof backpack that protects the items inside from water and impact damage.

About the product

Functional and practical design that is useful for both daily and emergency situations. The design specifically addresses the issues voiced by the target audience with regards to the functionality. The backack has a tough exo-skeleton to protect it from impact and a waterproof zipper to avoid water inlet. I have tried to achieve an IPX rating of 7 with this backack. The prototyping and testing was done within the budget limit provided by the university. The backpack was designed to hold a max capacity up to 25L, with multiple compartements for organization and an easily accessible closure.
